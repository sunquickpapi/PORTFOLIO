PPASRTUHUB Website

A comprehensive web platform dedicated for staff to manage facility bookings, resource allocation, and administrative workflows with integrated analytics.

My Role: Web Developer

The Challenge

Managing the diverse resources of the Perbadanan Perpustakaan Awam Selangor (PPAS)—from high-tech recording studios and auditoriums to basic inventory like tables and chairs—was operationally complex. The lack of a centralized digital system likely meant staff had to rely on manual tracking or disparate systems to handle facility bookings, event approvals, and news dissemination, leading to potential double-bookings and inefficient resource allocation across branches like PPAS Raja Tun Uda.

The Solution

Developed the PPAS Portal, a comprehensive web-based administration system designed to centralize library operations. The system features a Facility & Studio Management engine that efficiently handles specific venue types, such as "Bilik Kaca" and "Production Studios," with tiered pricing for students and the public alongside integrated capacity management. For physical assets, the Inventory Tracking module allows staff to precisely manage the stock and rental prices of movable items like extra chairs and tables required for large-scale events.

To keep the public informed, a built-in Content Management module enables staff to draft and publish real-time updates for major events like the Selangor International Book Fair directly to the site. All of these administrative functions are overseen via a centralized Analytics Dashboard, a visual command center featuring real-time charts for room booking frequency and facility trends that provide management with the data needed for informed decision-making.

🏆

Results & Impact

The portal successfully digitized the library's administrative workflow. By centralizing "Pending Approvals" and booking requests into a single dashboard, we significantly reduced administrative overhead and improved Operational Efficiency. Clear visibility into studio packages and facility usage ensured Revenue Optimization through better monetization of library assets, while the integration of automated analytics provided management with immediate insights for Data-Driven Resource Planning.

💡

Key Learnings

This project highlighted the importance of relational database design in facility management—specifically, how to link "Parent" assets (like a Hall) with "Child" inventory (like Chairs) to prevent resource conflicts. It also demonstrated the value of User Role Management (Staff vs. Admin) and the implementation of dynamic CRUD (Create, Read, Update, Delete) systems for diverse content types ranging from simple news text to complex studio booking slots.
