JobSeek Application

A mission-driven mobile platform designed to bridge the gap between inclusive talent and opportunity, featuring smart job matching and accessibility-first features.

My Role: Lead UI/UX Designer & Developer

The Challenge

The job market often presents invisible barriers to the disabled community, making traditional platforms feel exclusionary. Key challenges include a significant Information Asymmetry, where job seekers lack clarity on an employer's true inclusivity or available accommodations like screen reader support. Additionally, there are persistent Skills Gaps and a sense of Isolation, made worse by standard job boards that are rarely optimized for high-contrast viewing or assistive software.

The Solution

JobSeek is a comprehensive mobile platform designed to bridge the gap between talent and inclusive opportunity. I implemented Smart & Inclusive Job Search through a personalized Disability Profiling system—covering Visual, Hearing, Mobility, and Neurodivergence—that automatically filters roles. This is paired with Accommodation Transparency, where listings explicitly highlight verified inclusive features like captioned meetings or remote-only options.

To foster long-term success, the platform integrates Career Growth & Skills training where users can access tailored resources directly. This is supported by a robust Community Support system featuring specialized forums for different accessibility needs, allowing users to share real-world advice and step-by-step navigation instructions for the workplace.

🏆

Results & Impact

The platform transforms the job-hunting experience into a structured path for growth. We Streamlined Onboarding, allowing users to move from signup to verified recommendations in clicks, and Increased User Agency by giving them control over their disability profile. The High-Accessibility UI, featuring high-contrast and large-text modes, ensures that no user is locked out, truly delivering a holistic development tool that tracks applications alongside skill progress.

💡

Key Learnings

This project taught me that accessibility is not one-size-fits-all; designing for disability requires granular options that respect different needs. I realized that Transparency Builds Trust, as verified labels reduce the fear of disclosure during hiring. Above all, it highlighted that Community is a Career Tool, as peer validation provides applicants with a level of confidence that standard job descriptions simply cannot offer.
